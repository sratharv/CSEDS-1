Major Causes of Global Warming

1. Burning of Fossil Fuels

The combustion of coal, oil, and natural gas for electricity, heat, and transportation is the primary source of carbon dioxide emissions.

2. Deforestation and Land Use Change

Forests absorb large amounts of carbon dioxide. Cutting down trees for agriculture, timber, or urbanization reduces carbon storage and increases greenhouse gases.

3. Industrial Pollution

Factories release carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide during production processes such as cement, steel, and chemical manufacturing.

4. Agriculture & Livestock

Farming practices, especially livestock farming, release methane, while rice cultivation and fertilizer use emit nitrous oxide.

5. Waste Disposal and Landfills

Poor waste management and decomposition of organic matter in landfills release methane, a highly potent greenhouse gas.

6. Urbanization and Transportation

Growing cities and increased use of vehicles add to air pollution and carbon emissions, intensifying the greenhouse effect.

7. Population Growth

Increasing population leads to higher demand for energy, food, and land, accelerating deforestation, fossil fuel use, and greenhouse gas emissions.

8. Overconsumption of Resources

Excessive use of natural resources for industrial growth and modern lifestyles increases carbon footprint and worsens climate change.
